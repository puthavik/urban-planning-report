Books

Foundational Classics

The Death & Life of Great American Cities

Jane Jacobs — Seminal critique of mid-20th century planning. Advocates for vibrant, community-driven cities.

Cities of Tomorrow

Peter Hall — Comprehensive history of urban planning theories and their impact on modern cities.

The Power Broker

Robert Caro — Robert Moses and the interplay between politics and urban design.

A Pattern Language

Christopher Alexander — Timeless design principles for human-scale environments.

The Image of the City

Kevin Lynch — "Imageability" and how people perceive urban spaces.

Contemporary Reads (2024-2025)

Urbanism in the Age of Climate Change

Peter Calthorpe — Sustainable planning practices for addressing the climate crisis.

Key to the City: How Zoning Shapes Our World

Sara C. Bronin — Zoning's historical and modern impact on equity and sustainability.

Walkable City

Jeff Speck — Pedestrian-friendly cities as a pillar of sustainable urban design.

Inclusive Transportation

Veronica Davis — Intersection of transportation planning, accessibility, and social justice.

How to Kill a City

Peter Moskowitz — Gentrification, inequality, and the fight for neighborhoods.

Homelessness Is a Housing Problem

Gregg Colburn & Clayton Page Aldern — Structural causes of homelessness in US cities.

Building the Black City

Joe William Trotter Jr. — Development of Black "cities-within-cities" in the US.

The Ethics of Cities

Timothy Beatley — How ethics and sustainability intersect in urban policy.

Professional Organizations

Organization

American Planning Association (APA)

Largest planners' org (40,000+ members). Planning Magazine, 48 local chapters, National Planning Conference, job boards, mentoring.

Cost: $310-$671/year (income-based). Free for students.

planning.org →
Organization

Urban Land Institute (ULI)

Global nonprofit (48,000+ members). Urban Land Magazine, Knowledge Finder library, District Councils, global conferences.

Cost: Associate $540/yr | Full $1,332/yr

uli.org →
Organization

ICLEI — Local Govts for Sustainability

2,500+ local governments across 125+ countries. ClearPath Climate Planner, technical assistance, peer exchanges.

iclei.org →
🎓

Certifications

AICP — American Institute of Certified Planners

The leading professional credential for urban planners in the US, administered by APA.

Requirements: APA membership + education/experience prerequisites + comprehensive exam + ongoing professional development (CM credits every 2 years)

Benefits: Nationally recognized expertise validation, higher career prospects, exclusive resources & events

planning.org/aicp →
